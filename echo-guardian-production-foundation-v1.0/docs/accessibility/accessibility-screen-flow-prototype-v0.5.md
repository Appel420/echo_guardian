# Echo Guardian v0.5 Accessibility Screen-Flow Prototype

The v0.5 native accessibility skeleton must be understandable by a child, an elderly person, a caregiver, and a non-technical user.

## Screen 1 - Safety Status

Plain language: This screen tells you if Echo Guardian is watching for safety changes.

Voice prompt: What are you doing?

Expected answer: Echo Guardian monitoring is on or off, which spaces are active, what signals are being used, and who controls the setting.

## Screen 2 - Are You Okay?

Plain language: Echo Guardian is checking if you are okay. You can answer by voice, text, or touch.

Actions: I am okay. I need help. I cannot answer.

## Screen 3 - Alert Preview

Plain language: This shows exactly what your safety contact would receive.

The preview must use minimal disclosure and must never hide emergency-services status.

## Screen 4 - Degraded State

Plain language: Something is not working correctly. Echo Guardian will tell you what is affected and what still works.

No degraded state may be hidden.
